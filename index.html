What Is a Travel ID?

A travel ID is an identification document that allows you to travel both domestically and internationally. It contains your name, photograph, and other identifying information such as your date of birth, gender, and address.

If you plan to travel outside of the United States, you will need a passport. A passport is a travel ID that is issued by the federal government and is valid for 10 years. If you do not have a passport, you can apply for one at a passport acceptance facility.

If you are traveling within the United States, you may be able to use an alternative form of ID such as a driver’s license or state-issued ID card. However, some states now require that you have a travel ID to board an airplane.

Am I Required to Get an Arizona Travel ID?

If you plan to fly domestically, the answer is yes. The REAL ID Act was enacted by Congress and it set unique measures for the issuance of driver’s licenses and state-issued ID cards.

To board a domestic flight, you will need an ID that meets the requirements of the REAL ID Act. A driver’s license or state-issued ID card from a compliant state will work.

If your state is not in keeping with the REAL ID Act, you will need an alternative form of ID such as a passport. Arizona is not currently in keeping with the REAL ID Act, so if you plan to fly domestically, you will need an alternative form of ID.

The act set benchmarks for state-issued driver’s licenses and identification cards and required them to be more tamper-resistant and to include security features such as a digital photograph and Social Security number. The act also created a deadline for states to comply with the new standards.

Originally, that deadline was January 1, 2008. However, it has been pushed back several times, and the current deadline is May 2023. Fortunately, many states are already compliant with the REAL ID Act, so the extended majority of Americans will not be impacted by this change. What Are the Arizona Travel ID Requirements?

The requirements for an Arizona travel ID are the same as the requirements for any other state-issued ID. Let’s look at them in more detail:

  • You must be a U.S. citizen
  • You must have a Social Security number
  • You must have proof of your identities such as a birth certificate or passport
  • You must have two forms of documentation that show your Arizona address such as a utility bill or bank statement
  • You will need to provide proof of your name change if it is different from the name on your proof of identity documents
